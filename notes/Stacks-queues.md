Stacks return the most recently inserted data first, while queues return the oldest first.
The former is a core part of what is depth-first search, while the latter is for breadth-first.
Depth-first: deeper and deeper along a path until a dead end
Breadth-first: Shallow exploration of adjacent paths before digging deeper.

Stack: pile of paper (LIFO), two formal operations are push (add to top of stack) and pop
(remove from top of stack and return). Can be implemented with linked lists/arrays.

Queue: line at a shop (FIFO), two formal operations are enqueue (add to back of queue) and
dequeue (remove from front of queue and return).
